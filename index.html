The distributed training tax

How much extra compute does it cost to split a frontier training run across many sites? Efficiency model calibrated to Rahman (2026), Does Distributed Training Undermine Compute Governance? (arXiv:2605.29359), Tables 1 & 7.
Advanced assumptions

Across the range 10²⁶ – 10²⁸ FLOP

Model structure and assumptions
  • Modes. A: few large datacentre-class nodes (size set by the power cap; flat DiLoCo, dedicated fibre). B: many mid-size nodes small enough to pass as ordinary commercial buildings (~1–5 MW; flat or pipeline-parallel DiLoCo, good commercial internet). C: Rahman's threat model — 16-H100 nodes below every proposed monitoring threshold, on consumer internet. Pipeline depth S (modes B, C) and sync interval H are chosen by the model to minimise the tax.
  • Tax = nominal compute purchased ÷ quality-adjusted output, i.e. 1/(η·χ), following the paper: η = η_H·η_comp·η_rep·η_act, χ from Chinchilla suboptimality. The chip count, cost and power scale with the same factor.
  • Calibration. η_rep = exp(−k·ln M·ln H) with k = 0.07 + 0.004·ln M reproduces all six of Rahman's Table 1 configurations within ~5% on k (inferred), and the model's total η is within ~1.3–1.6× of the paper's across Tables 1 and 7. η_H = 1 − α·log₁₀H with α ≈ 0.05 at 250B, decreasing with model size (per the paper). η_comp = 0.99 at 150× compression (Covenant-72B calibration). η_act = 0.95 per pipeline boundary. χ = max(OT, 1/OT)^−0.35 fits the paper's reported (OT, χ) pairs.
  • Pipeline transfer. For pipeline depth > 1, compressed activations (forward) and activation-gradients (backward) must fit within per-site bandwidth: traffic ≈ token rate × 2 × hidden-dim ÷ compression (default 32×, adjustable — a key uncertainty). If it doesn't fit, throughput degrades proportionally (comm-bound stalls waste compute). Pipeline bubbles are neglected (large per-replica batches make them small); stragglers are not modelled. This constraint is why deep pipelines over slow links don't rescue small-node modes, and why Rahman's own configurations favour shallow pipelines on tiny nodes.
  • Sync interval and batch discipline. H is set to the minimum that keeps replicas compute-bound given per-site bandwidth (Streaming DiLoCo overlap; ring all-reduce; 16-bit pseudo-gradients at 150× compression). Fleet-wide tokens per step are capped at the critical batch size (default ~15M tokens at 100B params, scaling with √N — the √N form proxies for the scaling with training progress/data found by Zhang et al. 2024 and Merrill et al. 2025, since data scales with √compute); the optimiser may exceed the cap at a cost of 2/(1+f) (the McCandlish et al. steps–data trade-off — diminishing, not zero, marginal returns), or train an oversized model, at the modelled χ cost. Because H is fleet-determined under this discipline, splitting hardware into smaller nodes never helps — it only adds replicas. The critical-batch dropdown is the assumption that decides the result: Rahman's simulator implicitly allows unlimited batch parallelism (his GPT-5-scale configuration implies ~4 billion-token effective batches, ~100× beyond anything demonstrated, while the DiLoCo experiments his factors are calibrated on all stayed within the normal range). Setting the dropdown to 1–4B tokens approximately reproduces his regime; at demonstrated values, hidden-node distribution (modes B and C) plateaus well below the frontier.
  • The ≈1× regime is real but visible. With few sites, dark-fibre bandwidth and syncs every step at critical batch, the tax approaches 1 — this is ordinary multi-campus training (cf. Sevilla & Troynikov 2025, and how frontier labs already train), not covert distribution: it requires datacentre-class nodes on dedicated fibre.
  • Hardware. H100e at 2×10¹⁵ FLOP/s FP8 peak; 1.2 kW/chip all-in (Colossus 1 ratio); $50k installed per chip (Rahman's cost factors); ~14 bytes/param training memory. Mode C node memory is selectable (16×H100 1.28 TB / 16×GH200 2.3 TB / 50×A100 4 TB) since Rahman's hardware optimisation favours memory-rich sub-threshold nodes. Site counts shown are rounded up to whole sites; the solver itself treats them continuously.
  • Detectability threshold. The per-site "recognisable" flag is a heuristic cutoff (default 5 MW, adjustable in advanced settings), not a physical detection model — it proxies for the point where dense GPU-hall cooling infrastructure becomes legible in commercial satellite imagery (cf. Epoch's frontier-datacentre methodology). It affects labelling only, never the computed tax.
  • Caveats. Everything beyond ~10²⁶ FLOP and ~16 replicas extrapolates a simulator that itself extrapolates experiments at ≤16B parameters (largest real DiLoCo-family run: Covenant-72B, 4.8×10²³ FLOP). Chinchilla-optimal data volumes at 10²⁷⁺ exceed plausibly available unique tokens — a further unmodelled penalty. Algorithmic progress (better compression, divergence control) pushes taxes down over time.
